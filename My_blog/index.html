Ethiopia

  1. Ethiopia is the only African country that was never colonized.
  2. The Ethiopian calendar is about 7-8 years behind the Gregorian calendar.
  3. Coffee was discovered in Ethiopia! The legend says a goat herder named Kalid noticed his goats getting hyper after eating coffee berries.
  4. It's home to Lalibela, a city with 11 rock-hewn churches carved entirely from stone.

Kenya

  1. Kenya is the birthplace of long-distance running champions. The Kalenjin people are famous for dominating marathons.
  2. The country is home to the Great Migration, where over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles move across the Serengeti and Maasai Mara.
  3. Nairobi has the world’s only national park within a capital city—Nairobi National Park
  4. Kenya is where the term "safari" (meaning journey in Swahili) originated

Malawi

  1. Malawi is known as the “Warm Heart of Africa” because of its friendly people.
  2. Lake Malawi has more fish species than any other lake in the world.
  3. The country has an annual bicycle festival since bicycles are the main mode of transport.
  4. Malawi had one of the first female presidents in Africa, Joyce Banda.

Rwanda

  1. Rwanda is known as the “Land of a Thousand Hills” because of its rolling landscapes.
  2. It's the cleanest country in Africa—plastic bags were banned in 2008.
  3. The country has the highest percentage of women in parliament in the world.
  4. Kigali, the capital, is one of the safest cities in Africa.

South Sudan

  1. South Sudan is the youngest country in the world, gaining independence in 2011.
  2. The country has one of the biggest wetlands in Africa—the Sudd.
  3. Cattle play a huge role in culture, and many people paint their cattle’s horns in bright colors.
  4. South Sudan has over 60 indigenous ethnic groups, making it one of the most diverse places in Africa.

Somalia

  1. Somalia has the longest coastline in mainland Africa—about 3,333 km (2,070 miles).
  2. The country is known as the “Nation of Poets” because poetry is deeply rooted in its culture.
  3. Frankincense and myrrh, famous since ancient times, come from Somalia’s trees.
  4. Somalia has one of the highest camel populations in the world—used for milk, transport, and trade.

Uganda

  1. Uganda has the world's youngest population, with over 75% of its people under 30.
  2. It's one of the most bio-diverse countries, home to half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas.
  3. Lake Victoria, Africa’s largest lake, is shared by Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzania.
  4. The source of the Nile River is in Uganda, at Jinja.
